Understanding Forex: A Beginner's Guide

What is Forex

Forex, short for foreign exchange, is the global marketplace where national currencies are traded. Unlike physical cash that you carry in your wallet, forex trading happens entirely online through digital platforms. You don't "own" the currency in a physical sense instead, you speculate on its value relative to another currency, like EUR/USD (Euro and United State Dollar) or GBP/JPY (Great British Pound and Japaness Yen).
What makes forex unique is its massive scale and 24-hour accessibility. It's the largest financial market in the world, with over $7 trillion traded daily. Unlike centralized exchanges or government-issued assets, the forex market is decentralized meaning no single institution controls it. Instead, it operates through a global network of banks, brokers, and financial institutions. Thanks to this setup, traders can buy and sell currencies directly with each other or through brokers, without needing a central exchange. It's fast, global, and often more liquid than other financial markets.
